SoMa Stabilization Fund

Claudine has been leading the work of the SoMa Community Stabilization Fund since its inception in 2006. This unprecedented Fund was created to mitigate the impact of gentrification and displacement in the South of Market, one of the most rapidly developing neighborhoods in San Francisco. Over the years, Claudine has managed a process to disburse the funds to increase access to permanently affordable housing, support economic and workforce development, and strengthen community cohesion in SoMa. Small Sites Project

Through the SoMa Stabilization Fund, Claudine worked to help the community and the City acquire and preserve the Gran Oriente, an historic cultural asset that was purchased by a group of Filipino seamen in the 1920â€™s. The acquisition ensures that 24 units of SRO housing remain permanently affordable in a neighborhood that has been rapidly gentrified. In a community engagement process that involves meetings among the City, the nonprofit developer, and community leaders the goal is to honor the Filipino legacy of the building and create a community ownership model.
